TYPES OF SWIMMING

FRONT CRAWL (OR FREESTYLE)

It is the fastest and most common stroke. 1-You swim by alternating your arms and performing a flutter kick. 2-You breathe by turning your head to one side. 3-Backstroke (or dorsal) 4-You swim on your back. 5-Your arms move alternately, and your legs perform a kick similar to the crawl.

CHEST (OR STROKE)

It is slower but very technical. Both arms move forward and outward at the same time, while the legs make a circular kick. The body remains in a more horizontal position and the head comes up with each stroke.

3-BACKSTROKE

Backstroke is the only swimming style in which the swimmer floats on their back in the water, using alternating arm and leg movements to propel themselves forward. The movement is similar to freestyle, with the arms performing a circular stroke and the legs performing a crawl kick (backstroke kick).

BUTTERFLY

The butterfly stroke is characterized by a simultaneous, undulating movement of the arms and legs, similar to the flapping of a dolphin's tail.
